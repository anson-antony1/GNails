## What Is G Nail Growth?

G Nail Growth is your salon's all-in-one retention system. It helps you:

1. **Track every customer visit** - Check customers in when they arrive
2. **Get automatic feedback** - System sends SMS asking customers to rate their experience
3. **Catch problems early** - Low ratings create alerts so you can fix issues before they hit Yelp
4. **Drive Google reviews** - Happy customers (9-10/10) get guided to leave 5â˜… reviews
5. **Win back inactive clients** - Automatic SMS to customers who haven't returned

Think of it as having a marketing assistant who never forgets to follow up with customers.

## How It Works: The Complete Flow

### 1. Customer Visits Your Salon

**What happens:**
- Front desk staff logs into the **Check-in Console** (PIN protected)
- They enter customer's phone number
- System finds existing customer or creates new record
- Staff selects service and enters price
- Customer is checked in immediately

**Why it matters:** This single action triggers everything else automatically.

---

### 2. Automatic Feedback Request (30 Minutes Later)

**What happens:**
- 30 minutes after checkout, system sends SMS to customer
- Message example: *"Hi Sarah! This is G Nail Pines. Thanks for visiting today ðŸ’… We'd love a quick 1â€“10 rating about your visit: [link]"*
- Customer taps link and rates 1-10
- They can also leave a text comment

**Why it matters:** You get honest feedback while experience is fresh. Most customers respond within an hour.

---

### 3. System Response Based on Rating

#### If Rating is 1-6 (Problem Detected)
**What happens:**
- System creates an **Issue** in your Issues Inbox
- If customer left a comment, AI analyzes it and suggests:
  - Issue category (service quality, cleanliness, wait time, etc.)
  - Severity level (low/medium/high)
  - One-sentence summary
- You see customer name, phone, what they said, and when it happened

**What you do:**
- Review the issue in Issues Inbox
- Call or text the customer to apologize and offer remedy
- Mark issue as resolved once handled
- Learn from patterns (if multiple cleanliness complaints, investigate)

**Why it matters:** You find out about problems BEFORE they become public Google/Yelp reviews.

---

#### If Rating is 7-8 (Satisfied)
**What happens:**
- Feedback is recorded, no alerts
- Customer is thanked for their time

**What you do:** Nothing required. These customers are satisfied but not raving fans yet.

---

#### If Rating is 9-10 (Promoter!)
**What happens:**
- Customer gets immediate follow-up text: *"We're thrilled you had a great experience! ðŸŒŸ Would you mind sharing a quick review?"*
- Message includes buttons for both Google and Yelp reviews
- One tap takes them directly to review page

**Why it matters:** You capture promoters at peak satisfaction. Most 5â˜… reviews come from these customers.

---

### 4. Winback Inactive Customers (Automatic)

**What happens:**
- System identifies customers who haven't visited in 60+ days
- Sends friendly winback message: *"Hi Maria, we miss you at G Nail Pines! It's been about 65 days since your last visit. Reply to book your next appointment."*
- Won't spam - waits 30 days between messages to same customer

**Why it matters:** Reactivates 15-25% of inactive customers. Pure profit since they already know you.

## Settings You Can Customize

Go to **Settings** page to adjust business rules:

### Feedback Timing
**Feedback Delay (minutes)**: How long after checkout before SMS is sent
- Default: 30 minutes
- Recommendation: 15-60 minutes
- Why: Long enough customer left, soon enough they remember

### Issue Detection
**Low Rating Threshold**: What rating counts as a problem
- Default: 6 or below creates issue
- Recommendation: 6 or 7
- Why: 7+ means satisfied, 6- means unhappy

### Review Requests  
**Promoter Threshold**: Who gets asked for public review
- Default: 9 or 10 gets review request
- Recommendation: 9+
- Why: Only raving fans likely to leave 5â˜… review

### Winback Campaigns
**Inactive After (days)**: When customer is considered inactive
- Default: 60 days
- Recommendation: 45-90 days depending on typical visit frequency

**Cooldown Between Messages (days)**: Gap between winback attempts
- Default: 30 days
- Recommendation: 30-45 days
- Why: Prevents feeling spammy

### Review Links
- Set your actual Google Business and Yelp URLs
- Customers tap link â†’ lands directly on review page
- Critical for conversion

## Common Questions

### "Do I have to respond to every piece of feedback?"

**No.** Only low ratings (issues) require action. Ratings 7+ are recorded for analytics but don't need response.

---

### "What if customer doesn't have a phone or doesn't want texts?"

Front desk can still check them in - just skip the SMS. You'll have visit record but no automatic feedback.

---

### "Can I turn off feedback requests temporarily?"

Yes, in Settings change Feedback Delay to 0. This pauses feedback SMS but still tracks visits.

---

### "How do I know if system is working?"

Three checks:
1. **Today page** shows recent check-ins
2. **Dashboard** shows feedback coming in
3. **Issues Inbox** shows problems being caught

If check-ins happening but no feedback â†’ check Settings or contact support.

---

### "What if I get a really angry customer?"

1. Call them immediately (don't just text)
2. Listen and apologize sincerely  
3. Offer concrete remedy: refund, free redo, discount on next visit
4. Follow up 24 hours later to confirm they're satisfied
5. Mark issue Resolved only after they confirm

Most angry customers become loyalists if you handle well.

## Best Practices from Real Salons

### 1. Train Staff on Check-in
- Make sure every staff member knows their PIN
- Emphasize: checking in customer takes 20 seconds
- Show them how to handle new vs. returning customers

### 2. Respond to Issues Same Day
- Customers expect quick response (within 2-4 hours)
- Evening issues can wait until morning
- But morning issues should be handled by lunch

### 3. Review Your Dashboard Weekly
- Every Monday, look at week's feedback trends
- Spot patterns: specific staff getting complaints? Specific service?
- Address patterns in team meeting

### 4. Celebrate Wins
- Share positive feedback with team in morning huddle
- When customer leaves 5â˜… review, show whole team
- Creates culture of customer obsession

### 5. Trust the Numbers
- If average rating dropping, investigate
- If average rating high but visits down, focus on winback
- Data tells you where to focus effort
